Optimizing thermodynamical systems to properly save previously wasted energy has taken a great amount of attention since last decade. In this research, we have obtained mathematical equations which express energetic and exergetic efficiencies in terms of evaporator and generator temperatures for any reasonable working conditions, that is ambient, absorber, and condenser temperatures as well as effectiveness of heat exchanger. Having modeled an absorption cooling system (ACS) in MATLAB, I managed to use Genetic Algorithm to find the optimum evaporator and generator temperatures. Repeating the very optimization process, I have gathered a dataset of different working conditions and their optimum parameters. Using Group Method of Data Handling (GMDH) I have conducted a regression on available data and obtain mathematical equations which can be used in the process of designing ACSs. In the regression process, various structures have been considered for training the model with different activation function and hidden layer count. The first figure shows the schematics of ACS. The second one shows the structure of trained GMDH neural network, and the third one shows the result of sensitivity analysis on the model.
